A Little About Me

I'm a Brazilian-Korean-American Associate Professional Counselor and nationally board-certified Clinical Mental Health Counselor.

My academic journey includes a Master of Arts in Clinical Mental Health Counseling from Northwestern University and a Bachelor of Arts in Psychology from Georgia State University.

Fulfilling my lifelong dream of becoming a therapist, I have a profound belief in the transformative power of psychotherapy. Despite the effort it demands, the path is unquestionably rewarding. I place my confidence in the inherent power and strength within every client, fostering transformation from the inside out.

I value honesty, compassion, authenticity, inclusivity, and growth. It's a privilege to offer support, and I approach my work with professionalism and care.

How I Can Help

My goal is not to 'fix' your problems or talk you out of your emotions.

Instead, I'll guide you in understanding them, helping uncover their root causes for a deeper insight into yourself, your relationships, and behavior patterns. As your therapist, I prioritize creating a warm, non-judgmental space for you to process your experiences. I recognize the intricate layers beyond symptoms that contribute to your overall well-being.

I integrate evidence-based approaches such as Psychodynamic, Person-Centered, Cognitive-Behavioral, and Mindfulness practices. Continuously pursuing clinical training keeps me updated on the latest advancements in treatment. During our sessions, I'll invite you to explore how early experiences may influence your current behavior, consider the body-mind connection, and embrace the possibility of developing new thought patterns and practices for a more balanced life.

Although I cannot guarantee your results, I promise to offer you the best possible treatment options given your particular goals, and to work as hard as you are willing to work to help you achieve them.

I Help Individuals Experiencing

  • Anxiety & Stress

    The weight of anxiety and stress often manifests in both mind and body. In therapy, we explore the roots of these challenges, cultivating coping strategies and resilience to navigate life's uncertainties.

  • Depression

    Depression is not always a solitary battle; it intertwines with various aspects of life, affecting relationships, work, and self-perception. My approach to depression is rooted in empathy and tailored strategies, fostering a safe space for self-discovery and the emergence of hope in the face of darkness.

  • Racial Trauma

    The impact of racial trauma extends beyond the individual, touching the roots of identity, self-worth, and interpersonal connections. My therapeutic approach acknowledges the systemic forces that contribute to racial trauma, offering a collaborative space to process and confront these challenges while promoting emotional well-being.

  • Relationships & Intimacy

    Relationships form the canvas of our lives, and therapy is the palette where we explore the colors of intimacy. In therapy, we delve into the nuances of emotional connection, providing tools to enhance communication and deepen the bonds shared in your relationships.

  • LGBTQ+ Issues

    Issues of gender and sexuality are personal and unique; I strive to help you find and support the most authentic expression of yourself.

  • Trauma

    Trauma can cast a shadow on one's sense of self and the world. Together, we unravel the threads of pain, offering tools for coping, understanding, and reclaiming a sense of safety and well-being.

  • Identity Exploration

    Delve into the intricate layers of identity, offering a supportive space for self-discovery and understanding. Whether it's navigating questions of personal identity, cultural influences, or individual purpose, our therapeutic journey aims to foster a profound sense of self.

  • Family Dynamics

    Family is a complex tapestry of relationships and interactions. In therapy, we explore the dynamics at play, providing insights and strategies to enhance communication, resolve conflicts, and foster healthier connections within the family unit.

  • Perfectionism

    Perfectionism can be a relentless companion, affecting various aspects of life. Together, we unravel the patterns of perfectionism, cultivating self-compassion and resilience to navigate challenges and embrace imperfections.

  • Self-Esteem

    Building a positive self-image is a transformative process. Our therapeutic exploration focuses on understanding and reshaping self-perceptions, fostering self-acceptance, and empowering you to embrace your unique strengths.

What I Offer

Typically, sessions occur on a weekly basis, though the frequency can be adjusted based on scheduling needs and individual preferences.

While I do not accept insurance, I can offer super-bills, and if you have a PPO or a related plan, you may qualify for partial reimbursement.
